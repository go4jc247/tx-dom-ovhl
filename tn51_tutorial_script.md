# Tennessee 51 Tutorial — Narration Script

Voice: Arthur - Engaging Southern Cowboy (ElevenLabs)
Format: Same as T42 tutorial — ellipses for pauses, spelled-out numbers, colons for emphasis

## LESSON 1: The Dominoes (4 steps)

**L1_S1_welcome**
"Welcome to: Tennessee fifty-one Training! ... This tutorial will teach you a bigger, bolder domino game... six players, three teams, and fifty-one points on the line! So pull up a chair, partner... there's room for everyone."

**L1_S2_domino_set**
"Tennessee fifty-one uses a double-seven domino set... that's thirty-six dominoes total. Each domino has two ends, with a number from zero to seven on each side. For example... double-seven, six-five, three-blank."

**L1_S3_six_players**
"Six players sit around the table. All thirty-six dominoes get dealt out... six each. You sit at the bottom. Your partner sits directly across from you at the top. And those four other players? ... They're on two other teams."

**L1_S4_deal**
"Here are your six dominoes at the bottom of the screen. Everyone else's dominoes are face-down. Go ahead... tap any of your dominoes to see how they sort!"

---

## LESSON 2: Teams & Seating (3 steps)

**L2_S1_three_teams**
"Three teams! ... Tennessee fifty-one has three teams of two players each. You and your partner across the table are Team one. The players to your left and right... each paired with the player across from them... that's Teams two and three."

**L2_S2_team_dynamics**
"Here's where it gets interesting... when one team wins the bid, the other two teams become defenders. Those two defender teams work together to stop the bidder! It's always two against one... team-wise."

**L2_S3_cooperation**
"That means four players are trying to set the bidding team, and only two are trying to make it. The bidder and their partner have to play sharp... because the odds are stacked against them!"

---

## LESSON 3: Suits & Doubles (4 steps)

**L3_S1_suits**
"Suits! ... With a double-seven set, there are eight suits: blanks, aces, deuces, treys, fours, fives, sixes, and sevens. A domino belongs to a suit if it has that number on either end."

**L3_S2_sevens_suit**
"Take the sevens suit for example... it has: Double-seven, seven-six, seven-five, seven-four, seven-trey, seven-deuce, seven-ace, and seven-blank. That's eight dominoes in every suit. And the double... is always the highest. Double-seven is king of the sevens!"

**L3_S3_doubles**
"Now doubles... doubles are special. Double-seven is the highest seven. Double-six is the highest six. Double-five is the highest five... all the way down to double-blank, which is the highest blank. The lowest domino in any suit is the one with blank on the other end."

**L3_S4_two_suits**
"Each domino belongs to two suits. The seven-four? That belongs to both the sevens and the fours. When a suit is led in a trick, you must follow that specific suit. More on that when we start playing!"

---

## LESSON 4: Count Dominoes (3 steps)

**L4_S1_count_tiles**
"Count dominoes! ... Some dominoes are worth extra points. The five-count dominoes are worth five points each: five-blank, four-ace, and trey-deuce. The ten-count dominoes are worth ten points each: double-five, six-four, and seven-trey. That's one more ten-count than in forty-two... remember that!"

**L4_S2_total_count**
"Let's add it up... three five-counts at five points each... that's fifteen. Three ten-counts at ten points each... that's thirty. Fifteen plus thirty equals forty-five points of count. Now add one point for each of the six tricks... forty-five plus six equals... fifty-one! And that, partner... is where the name comes from!"

**L4_S3_your_count**
"Now look at your hand... seven-trey, that's ten count. Double-five, another ten count. Five-blank is five count. Four-ace is five more. That's thirty points of count in your hand! ... Very strong."

---

## LESSON 5: Bidding & Trump (6 steps)

**L5_S1_bidding**
"The bidding phase! ... After dealing, players bid clockwise starting left of the shuffler. You're bidding on how many of those fifty-one points your team can win. Minimum bid is thirty-four... maximum is fifty-one. And if your hand ain't looking too good... you can always pass."

**L5_S2_highest_bid**
"The highest bidder wins the bid... and gets to choose the trump suit. If everybody passes? ... The dominoes get reshuffled and we start over. Bidding thirty-four means your team needs thirty-four out of fifty-one points to earn a mark."

**L5_S3_when_to_bid**
"Here's a good rule of thumb for when to bid thirty-four... You want at least three dominoes in your best suit, including the double... plus two other doubles on the side. For example: double-seven, seven-four, seven-deuce... plus double-trey and double-five. That's a solid thirty-four bid right there!"

**L5_S4_your_hand**
"Look at your hand... double-seven, seven-trey, seven-deuce... that's three sevens with the double! Plus double-five and double-trey on the side. That's a solid thirty-four-bid hand, partner! ... Let's bid."

**L5_S5_bid**
"Use the slider to set thirty-four... then tap Bid. The other players will pass."

**L5_S6_trump**
"You won the bid! ... Now pick your trump suit. You've got three sevens with the big double-seven. Slide over to sevens... then tap Confirm Trump."

---

## LESSON 6: Playing Tricks (4 steps)

**L6_S1_tricks**
"How tricks work! ... The bid winner leads the first trick. Each player follows clockwise... that's six players playing one domino each. Now listen up... you must follow the led suit if you can. Can't follow suit? ... Play anything you like... including trump!"

**L6_S2_who_wins**
"Who wins the trick? ... If any trump was played, the highest trump takes it. Otherwise... the highest domino in the led suit wins. The winner leads the next trick. After six tricks, we count up the points."

**L6_S3_strategy**
"Strategy tip! ... Lead with your strong trumps first to pull trump from all four opponents! Double-seven can't be beaten by anyone. This gets their trumps out of the way... and wins you the trick at the same time. Now let's play!"

**L6_S4_play**
"You lead first! Click a domino to play it. Try leading with your strongest trump!"

---

## LESSON 7: Scoring (3 steps)

**L7_S1_scoring**
"Scoring! ... After six tricks, count up your team's points... that's tricks won plus any count dominoes you captured. Did the bidding team make their bid? ... They earn one mark. Fall short? ... That's called getting set... and the other two teams each get one mark! First team to seven marks wins the whole game!"

**L7_S2_example**
"Here's an example... you bid thirty-four with sevens trump. Your team won four tricks... that's four points. Plus you captured double-five for ten, seven-trey for ten, and four-ace for five. Four plus ten plus ten plus five equals... twenty-nine. Uh oh... that's a set, partner! Both opponent teams get a mark."

**L7_S3_key_facts**
"Key facts to remember... each trick is worth one point. Five-blank, four-ace, and trey-deuce are five count each. Double-five, six-four, and seven-trey are ten count each. Fifty-one total points available every hand. One mark per hand won... seven marks to win the game. Both you and your partner's tricks count together!"

---

## LESSON 8: Following Suit (3 steps)

**L8_S1_following**
"Following suit! ... When someone leads a domino, the higher number determines the suit. If a player leads five-trey... that's the fives suit. You must play a five if you have one! No fives? ... Play anything... throw in a trump to steal the trick!"

**L8_S2_trumping**
"Trumping a trick! ... When you can't follow suit, playing a trump wins. But be careful... if multiple players trump, the highest trump wins. And with six players in the mix... there's a better chance someone else has trump too!"

**L8_S3_trump_led**
"When trump is led... everyone must play trump if they've got one. No trump left? Play whatever you like... but you can't win that trick. This is exactly why leading trumps early is so powerful... it gets all four opponents' trumps and drains them dry!"

---

## LESSON 9: Practice Hand (5 steps)

**L9_S1_practice**
"Practice time! ... Full hand: deal, bid, pick trump, play all six tricks, then score. Ready, partner?"

**L9_S2_deal**
"Study your hand... look for trump candidates, doubles, and count dominoes. Bid when you're ready!"

**L9_S3_bid**
"Look at that... four fives with double-five! Plus double-four and some count dominoes. Bid thirty-four with fives as your trump."

**L9_S4_trump**
"Pick fives as trump... you've got four of them including the big double!"

**L9_S5_play**
"Play all six tricks! Lead with your strong trumps first to pull them in. Double-five can't be beat. Seven-trey is ten-count... and it's also in your trump suit if you play it right."

## LESSON 10: Nello (3 steps)

**L10_S1_nello**
"Special bid: Nello! ... This one's a gamble, partner. You're telling the table: I will win zero tricks! It's available when you bid the maximum... fifty-one. Pull it off with zero tricks won... your team earns a mark. But win even one trick? ... The other teams get the mark."

**L10_S2_nello_rules**
"Nello rules! Your partner sits out... it's you against the other five players. No trump suit. You lead first. And you must avoid winning any trick. Nello hands need lots of low dominoes."

**L10_S3_good_nello**
"A good Nello hand has lots of blanks and low numbers... no doubles, because doubles are the highest in their suit. Something like: ace-blank, deuce-blank, trey-blank, four-blank, deuce-ace, trey-ace. All very low... real hard to win any trick with those!"

---

## LESSON 11: Doubles as Trump (3 steps)

**L11_S1_doubles_trump**
"Special trump: Doubles! ... Instead of picking a regular suit as trump, you can declare all doubles as trump! Double-seven is the highest trump... then double-six, double-five, double-four, all the way down to double-blank. That's eight trump dominoes... one hiding in every suit."

**L11_S2_when_doubles**
"When to bid doubles? ... You want four or more doubles, ideally five. Something like: double-seven, double-five, double-trey, double-deuce, double-ace... plus five-blank and seven-four. Five doubles! Bid thirty-four with doubles as trump. Double-seven pulls doubles from everybody... very powerful."

**L11_S3_follow_me**
"Doubles follow me! ... When doubles are trump and you lead a double, your opponents must play a double if they have one. This house rule is on by default... and it makes doubles-as-trump a mighty strong play!"

---

## LESSON 12: Final Challenge (7 steps)

**L12_S1_review**
"You've learned the basics! ... Let's review: thirty-six dominoes, six each, six players, three teams. Bid thirty-four to fifty-one on the points your team can win. Pick trump... your strongest suit. Follow suit, use trumps strategically. Forty-five count plus six tricks equals fifty-one. First team to seven marks wins!"

**L12_S2_reference**
"Quick reference! ... Five-count dominoes: five-blank, four-ace, trey-deuce. Ten-count dominoes: double-five, six-four, and seven-trey. To bid thirty-four: three trumps with the double, plus two side doubles. Lead strong trumps early! And always... protect your count dominoes!"

**L12_S3_deal**
"Final challenge! Study your hand, bid smart, and play to win. Good luck, partner!"

**L12_S4_bid**
"Look at that hand... four fours with the double! Plus double-seven for off-suit dominance, and trey-deuce and five-blank for ten points of count. Bid thirty-four with confidence!"

**L12_S5_trump**
"Pick fours as trump... you've got four of them plus the double! And double-seven will dominate any off-suit trick."

**L12_S6_play**
"Lead your trumps, play double-seven for off-suit tricks. Protect five-blank and trey-deuce... those are five count each! ... This is your final test, partner. Make us proud!"

**L12_S7_congrats**
"Congratulations! ... You've completed the Tennessee fifty-one Tutorial! You're ready to play for real now, partner. Hit New Game... and choose Tennessee fifty-one! ... Good luck out there."
